Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the slideshow file take so long to open?
The HTML slideshow packs over 200 photos and 4 music files inside a single file. The very first time you open it, your browser saves all of those images into a local database called IndexedDB. This one-time setup takes about 10–20 seconds. After that, the file opens almost instantly every time — no internet required!
Do I need the internet to use these files after downloading?
No! Once downloaded, both files work completely offline. The HTML slideshow caches everything in your browser's local storage, and the MP4 video is a standalone file. You can watch them on a plane, at grandma's house, or anywhere.
Which browser should I use for the slideshow?
The slideshow works in any modern browser. We recommend Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge for the best experience. Safari and Firefox also work great. Just make sure your browser is up to date.
How do I navigate the interactive slideshow?
The slideshow has two modes: Slideshow Mode auto-plays all photos with music — just press play and relax. Explore Mode lets you freely browse: use your scroll wheel (or pinch on a touchscreen) to zoom, and click-and-drag to pan around the full canvas of photos.
Can I share these files with grandparents or relatives?
Absolutely! You're welcome to share the Google Drive folder link with immediate family. Each person will need to download the files separately to their own device. The files are meant as personal keepsakes for each family.
The slideshow seems stuck or blank — what do I do?
First, give it the full 10–20 seconds on the initial load. If it still appears stuck, try: (1) refreshing the page, (2) opening the file in a different browser (Chrome usually works best), or (3) clearing your browser's site data and re-opening the file to trigger a fresh load.
How large are the files?
The HTML slideshow file is compact since images load on first open, but the initial download to your browser's storage requires a good internet connection for the first-time setup. The MP4 video is a larger file. Both are worth the download — these memories will last a lifetime! 🌟
Can I watch the video on my TV?
Yes! Since the video is a standard MP4 file, you can cast it to a smart TV using Chromecast or AirPlay, connect your laptop via HDMI, or use a USB drive to play it directly on most modern smart TVs. It's a wonderful experience on the big screen!
