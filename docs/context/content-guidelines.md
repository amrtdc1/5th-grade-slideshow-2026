# Content Guidelines

## Tone
Use a tone that is:
- warm
- welcoming
- clear
- school-appropriate
- concise

## Writing style
- Prefer short sentences.
- Prefer short paragraphs.
- Use descriptive headings.
- Make important actions easy to scan.
- Avoid jargon and overly technical language in user-facing copy.

## Calls to action
Calls to action should:
- clearly tell the visitor what to do,
- be specific,
- appear prominently,
- avoid vague labels like "Click here" when better wording is available.

Preferred examples:
- View the 2026 5th Grade Slide Show
- Open the slideshow
- See the class slideshow

## Privacy and safety
- Do not include private student information.
- Do not publish personal contact details unless explicitly intended and approved.
- Avoid content that identifies minors in sensitive ways.
- Be careful with photos, names, and personal references.

## Formatting guidance
- Keep pages easy to scan.
- Use headings and spacing to separate sections.
- Avoid large blocks of text.
- Use bullet points when they improve readability.

## Consistency guidance
- Refer to the project consistently as the “2026 5th Grade Slide Show” unless a different official title is provided.
- Keep capitalization and button labels consistent.
- Reuse phrasing for repeated actions where appropriate.

## Editing guidance for contributors and agents
Before publishing content changes, check:
- Is the purpose of the page obvious?
- Is the main action easy to find?
- Is the wording concise?
- Is the tone appropriate for families and school audiences?
- Does the content avoid private or sensitive information?
